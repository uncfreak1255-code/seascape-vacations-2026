An Exclusive Coastal Retreat

Longboat Key is a stunning 11-mile barrier island that separates the calm waters of Sarasota Bay from the shimmering Gulf of Mexico. Renowned for its elegant, upscale atmosphere, it offers a distinctly different vibe from its neighbors to the north and south.

Known for perfectly manicured landscapes, championship golf courses, and premier tennis facilities, Longboat Key appeals to travelers seeking a sophisticated and serene getaway. The beaches here are quieter, offering uninterrupted relaxation and spectacular sunsets far from the crowds.

When you stay on Longboat Key, you enjoy the best of both worlds: complete island tranquility combined with incredibly easy access to world-class dining and shopping. The southern end of the key connects directly to St. Armands Circle and downtown Sarasota, making it effortless to enjoy cultural events, fine dining, and boutique shopping before retreating to your peaceful beachfront or bay-front rental.

Longboat Key FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is Longboat Key located?

Longboat Key stretches for 11 miles between Anna Maria Island to the north and Lido Key/St. Armands Circle to the south, effectively separating Sarasota Bay from the Gulf of Mexico.

How is Longboat Key different from Anna Maria Island?

While Anna Maria Island is known for its casual, 'Old Florida' charm and bustling shop districts, Longboat Key offers a highly upscale, resort-style atmosphere. It features manicured landscapes, quiet residential stretches, and world-class golf and tennis facilities.

What is near Longboat Key?

At the southern end of the island, visitors have immediate access to St. Armands Circle, world-renowned for its high-end shopping and dining. Downtown Sarasota, with its cultural attractions and arts scene, is just minutes across the Ringling Bridge.

Are there public beaches on Longboat Key?

The beaches on Longboat Key are pristine and much quieter than surrounding areas. While public beach accesses exist, parking is very limited, which largely preserves the beaches for residents and those staying in vacation rentals on the island.
