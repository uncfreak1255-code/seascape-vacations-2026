25 Best Things to Do
in Bradenton, Florida

A local's guide to the best activities, hidden gems, and must-see attractions in Bradenton and the surrounding Gulf Coast.

Bradenton sits on Florida's Gulf Coast between Tampa and Sarasota, offering everything from pristine beaches to art galleries ‚Äî without the tourist crowds. Whether you're visiting for a week or just planning a day trip from Anna Maria Island, here are the 25 best things to do.

1. Anna Maria Island Beaches

FREE Family 10 min from Bradenton

Anna Maria Island is just across the bridge. Bean Point offers solitude, Manatee Beach has the best facilities, and Coquina Beach is perfect for families. Read our full AMI beaches guide ‚Üí

2. Kayak Robinson Preserve

FREE Nature Northwest Bradenton

682 acres of restored coastal habitat with a free kayak launch, observation tower, and miles of trails winding through mangroves. Bring your own kayak or rent nearby. Best at sunrise when the birds are most active.

3. Paddleboard the Manatee River

$30-50 Active Downtown waterfront

Rent a SUP from local outfitters along the Riverwalk. Morning sessions are calmest. You'll likely spot dolphins, manatees, and rays right from your board.

4. Charter a Fishing Trip

$300-600 All Levels

Bradenton is a fishing paradise. Inshore trips target snook, redfish, and trout in the flats. Offshore charters head for grouper, snapper, and kingfish. See our complete fishing guide ‚Üí

5. Spot Dolphins & Manatees

FREE-$50 Family

Watch dolphins play from the Palma Sola Causeway, or book a guided eco-tour for guaranteed sightings. Winter months bring manatees to warm-water areas throughout the river system. Our wildlife guide ‚Üí

üé® Arts, Culture & History

6. Explore the Village of the Arts

FREE Culture Downtown Bradenton

A colorful neighborhood of artist studios, galleries, and cafes in restored bungalows. First Friday Art Walks (5-9pm) are the best time to visit ‚Äî meet the artists, enjoy live music, and explore open studios.

7. De Soto National Memorial

FREE History Northwest Bradenton

Commemorating Hernando de Soto's 1539 landing. Free ranger-led programs, living history demonstrations (Dec-April), a short nature trail, and a small beach. One of Florida's most underrated national park sites.

8. Bishop Museum of Science and Nature

$22 Family

Natural history museum with a planetarium, manatee rehabilitation habitat, and the Mosaic Backyard Universe for kids. The Sea Biscuit submarine replica is a hit with children.

9. Walk the Riverwalk

FREE Scenic Downtown

1.5-mile paved path along the Manatee River with public art, benches, and waterfront views. Connects to restaurants and the Bradenton Farmers' Market (Saturdays, Oct-May).

üåø Parks & Nature

10. Emerson Point Preserve

FREE Nature Palmetto

365 acres at the western tip of Snead Island with hiking trails, ancient Native American mounds, mangrove tunnels for kayaking, and panoramic views of Tampa Bay and the Gulf. One of our top recommendations.

11. Palma Sola Botanical Park

FREE Peaceful

10-acre garden with native plants, walking paths, butterfly gardens, and a bamboo forest. A quiet morning walk here is the perfect start to any day.

12. Neal Preserve

FREE Hiking

120-acre coastal preserve with an observation platform overlooking Sarasota Bay. Less crowded than Robinson Preserve with excellent birding opportunities.

üçΩÔ∏è Food & Drink

13. Dine on the Waterfront

$$-$$$ Dining

Bradenton's waterfront dining scene is excellent. Pier 22 on the Manatee River is the standout for fine dining. Ortygia for Italian, and Oak & Stone for craft beer and pizza. For casual, hit the Rod & Reel Pier on AMI.

14. Bradenton Farmers' Market

FREE Saturday Oct-May

Downtown's Old Main Street comes alive with 40+ vendors selling tropical fruits, local honey, fresh seafood, baked goods, and artisan crafts. Live music and food trucks round out the experience.

15. Craft Brewery Tour

$5-8/beer 21+

Bradenton has a growing craft beer scene. Motorworks Brewing (in a historic ice plant) is the flagship, with Darwin Brewing and 3 Keys Brewing also worth visiting.

‚öΩ Sports & Adventure

16. IMG Academy

Varies Sports

The world's most prestigious multi-sport training facility. Watch future pros train, catch exhibition matches, or book a golf round at the Legacy Golf Club. A unique Bradenton-only experience.

17. Rent Bikes on AMI

$15-30/day Family

Anna Maria Island is flat and bike-friendly. Rent cruisers and ride the 7-mile island from Bean Point to Coquina Beach, stopping for ice cream and pier fishing along the way.

18. Jet Ski the Bay

$80-150/hr Thrill

Guided jet ski tours explore the waterways around AMI and Longboat Key. Dolphin encounters are nearly guaranteed. Several outfitters operate from Holmes Beach and Bradenton Beach.

45 min Day Trip

FREE Nature
