Bradenton Beach
Anna Maria Island

AMI's charming southern village ‚Äî home to historic Bridge Street, pet-friendly Coquina Beach, and the island's most authentic Old Florida atmosphere.

Bradenton Beach is Anna Maria Island's quiet southern anchor ‚Äî a small, walkable village where historic Bridge Street meets the Gulf of Mexico. While Holmes Beach draws the crowds and Anna Maria City attracts the sunset seekers, Bradenton Beach offers something unique: the feeling that you've stepped back in time to Old Florida.

With Coquina Beach (one of AMI's best), the charming Bridge Street pier, and easy access to the mainland, Bradenton Beach is an underrated gem for travelers who want authenticity over glitz.

Beaches in Bradenton Beach

Coquina Beach

Coquina Beach is the crown jewel of Bradenton Beach ‚Äî a wide, beautiful stretch at the southern tip of AMI with free parking, picnic pavilions, a beach caf√©, restrooms, and outdoor showers. It's noticeably less crowded than Manatee Beach to the north, making it a favorite among locals.

Pet owners take note: Coquina Beach is one of the most pet-friendly beaches on AMI. Leashed dogs are welcome, and there's even a nearby dog park. If you're traveling with your four-legged family member, this is your beach.

Cortez Beach

North of Coquina, Cortez Beach offers a quieter alternative with numbered public access points along Gulf Drive. The sand is the same soft, white Gulf Coast variety ‚Äî just without the crowds.

Leffis Key Nature Preserve

Adjacent to Coquina Beach, Leffis Key is a hidden gem that most visitors miss. This nature preserve offers:

  • Paved and boardwalk trails through coastal mangrove habitat
  • A two-story observation tower with panoramic views of Sarasota Bay
  • Kayak and paddleboard launch point for calm bay waters
  • Excellent birdwatching ‚Äî herons, ospreys, pelicans, and roseate spoonbills
  • Fishing access along the bay shoreline
