Anna Maria Island

Holmes Beach is the largest and most vibrant of Anna Maria Island's three cities. Occupying the central stretch of this seven-mile barrier island, it's where you'll find the most restaurants, shops, and vacation rental options. If AMI is Florida's best-kept beach secret, Holmes Beach is its beating heart.

Beaches in Holmes Beach

Manatee Beach Park

The island's most popular and accessible beach. Manatee Beach has the largest parking lot on AMI (both free and paid), restrooms, outdoor showers, a playground, volleyball courts, and the on-site Caf√© on the Beach restaurant. It's the go-to spot for families and first-time visitors.

Holmes Beach Public Access Points

Beyond Manatee Beach, dozens of numbered beach access paths lead from Gulf Drive to the sand. These spots are quieter and perfect for those who want a more private beach experience. Just look for the blue "Beach Access" signs along Gulf Drive ‚Äî bring your own chairs since there are no facilities at these walk-overs.

Holmes Beach vs. Other AMI Neighborhoods

Anna Maria Island has three incorporated cities, each with its own personality:

  • Holmes Beach (central) ‚Äî The most options for dining, shopping, and rentals. Livelier atmosphere. Best beach access at Manatee Beach.
  • Anna Maria City (north) ‚Äî Quieter, more residential. Home to the Rod & Reel Pier and secluded Bean Point. Old Florida charm at its finest.
  • Bradenton Beach (south) ‚Äî Smallest city. Historic Bridge Street district with shops and restaurants. Closest to Coquina Beach and Bradenton.

üè° Where Should You Stay?

Choose Holmes Beach if you want walkable restaurants, the most rental options, and a central location to explore the whole island. For a quieter, more secluded experience, consider Anna Maria City. For proximity to the mainland and Coquina Beach, Bradenton Beach is your best bet.
